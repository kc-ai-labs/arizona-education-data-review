How to read this page (no statistics background needed)

Filters define the comparison group. County and subject change which schools appear in the aggregate cards, top movement lists, and school selector. Test level, subgroup, and FAY status stay visible for auditability; the current dataset has one value for each.

Weighted aggregate. Picture pooling every tested student from the selected schools into one big group, then finding that whole group's passing rate. Schools that tested more students pull the average more, because they brought more students into the pool. The "Weights" chip shows how many schools have a recent tested-student count to pool in; schools missing that number sit out of this calculation.

Quick example. Two schools take the Math test: one at 48% passing with 111 students tested, and a larger school at 80% passing with 1,000 students tested. Give each school one equal vote and the average is (48 + 80) ÷ 2 = 64%. Weight by students tested and it is (48×111 + 80×1,000) ÷ (111 + 1,000) = 76.8%. The weighted number sits closer to 80% because that larger school tested about nine times as many students. Weighted = students vote; Median-School = schools vote.

Median school. This gives every school one vote and reports the middle school in the selected group. Use it when you want the typical school, not the tested-student-weighted average.

Group trend chart. The chart plots the same group over time. The bold line is the weighted aggregate (students vote); the lighter line is the median school (schools vote). Solid segments are actual years, the dashed segment is the FY2026 indicator, and the sparse-dotted segment is the FY2030 illustration. Each actual year is weighted by that year's tested-student counts, so the chart can move slightly differently from any single card.

FY2026 and FY2030. FY2026 carries the latest actual score forward because that won the FY2025 backtest. FY2030 is an illustration of where the group or school would land if the recent trend continued; it is not a decision-ready forecast.

Top movement lists. Increases and decreases rank schools by movement from latest actual to FY2030. These are review prompts for spotting patterns worth discussing, not causal claims about what produced the trend.

Tested Students. This is simply how many students that school tested in its most recent year. A value of 111 means 111 students took the assessment there that year, and in the weighted average that school's score counts with the weight of 111 students. A school that tested 222 counts about twice as much; one that tested 55 counts about half as much. The Median-School cards ignore size and give every school one equal vote.

Compare schools. Add up to 10 schools for side-by-side review. Selected schools do not redefine the aggregate cards; the cards still summarize the full filtered group.
